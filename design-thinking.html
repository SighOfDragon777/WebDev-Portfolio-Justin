💡 Design Thinking Process

How I applied human-centered design to build this portfolio — inspired by F1 engineering, mechanical problem-solving, and user empathy.

1

Empathize

I put myself in the shoes of recruiters, peers, and potential collaborators. They want:

  • Clean, fast navigation
  • Accessible content (screen reader friendly)
  • A genuine sense of who I am — not just a template

F1 connection: Like an engineer designing a car for the driver, I designed this site for the user's experience.

2

Define

Problem: Many portfolios look generic and lack personality. They don't tell a story about who you are.

Solution: A responsive, accessible site that showcases my technical skills AND my three passions: F1, Mechanical Engineering, and Reading.

3

Ideate

I sketched 3 different layouts on paper (yes, paper!). I decided on:

  • Hero section with photo + introduction
  • 3-card grid for my hobbies (F1, Engineering, Books)
  • UN Goal #7 (Clean Energy) — tied to engineering
  • Accessibility statement to show I care about inclusion

Color palette inspired by: Ferrari Red (#E63946) + Engineering Blue (#1E3A5F) + Clean Energy Teal (#2A9D8F)

4

Prototype

I built the website step by step:

  • HTML structure first (semantic tags: header, nav, main, section, footer)
  • CSS for styling (Grid for portfolio, Flexbox for navbar)
  • JavaScript for interactivity (form validation + dark mode toggle)

I treated this like building a mechanical prototype — test early, iterate often.

5

Test

I tested on multiple browsers:

  • Chrome, Firefox, Edge
  • Used Chrome DevTools to simulate iPhone 12, iPad, and Pixel 5
  • Fixed mobile menu bugs and contrast issues

Just like testing an engine: You find the problems before the final lap.

📖 How Reading Influenced My Design

Reading "The Design of Everyday Things" by Don Norman taught me that good design is invisible. Users shouldn't have to think about how to navigate — it should feel natural. That's why I used familiar patterns (top navbar, card grid, footer) and added clear hover states.
